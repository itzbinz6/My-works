Our Story

Born from Grandmother's Kitchen

Aso Òkè began as a single question: what if Nigeria's most beloved dishes were given the stage they always deserved?

Our head chef, Adaeze Okonkwo, spent three years travelling from Lagos to Calabar, Kano to Enugu — learning, tasting, and preserving recipes that have fed generations. Every dish on our menu carries that history.

The name itself — Aso Òkè — means fine cloth in Yoruba. A fabric worn for celebrations, for moments that matter. We believe dinner should feel exactly that way.
