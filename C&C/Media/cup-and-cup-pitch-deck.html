How To Pitch This

What to say
when you reach out.

Open
Lead with the compliment, not the sell
"Hi! I've been following @cup_n_cup_plano for a while and honestly your drinks are some of the most beautiful boba content I've seen in Plano. The pistachio milk tea photos? Obsessed."
Bridge
State the problem they already feel
"I noticed you guys have over 500 five-star reviews on Google, which is incredible — but I couldn't find a website that really shows off what you do. I think you're leaving a lot of first-time customers on the table."
Show
Present the work — let it speak first
"I put together a concept for you — your full menu, real customer reviews with linked keywords, your hours, Instagram, and Yelp, all in one page. Can I send you the link to take a look?"
Anchor
Set the value before the price
"A page like this — custom-built, mobile-optimised, with your reviews and full menu — typically runs between [your range]. I'm open to talking through what works for you. What matters most is that it's actually useful for your business."

The Close

Three ways
this can go.

Have a clear ask ready for each possible response they give you. Don't wing it.

A
They love itMove to pricing. Have a range ready — not a single number. Anchor high, leave room to negotiate. Get a next-step date locked in before you leave the conversation.
B
They want changesGreat sign — they're engaged. Note exactly what they want changed. That's your revision scope. Come back with an updated quote that includes those changes.
C
They pass or need timeRespect it. Ask if you can check back in 2–3 weeks. Leave the demo link with them. Businesses revisit decisions — the warmest "no" often becomes a "yes" later.
